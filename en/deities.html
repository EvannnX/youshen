Deity Field Guide

Roles, lineages, faces, costumes, and procession positions

SCROLL

The Youshen pantheon is not a static list of gods. It is a working ritual cast: plague-expelling emperors, local protectors, underworld messengers, martial guardians, attendants, and newly beloved prince figures.

I

The Five Emperors

Core Plague-Expelling Deities

A central pillar of the Fuzhou Youshen belief system

From Disease to Protection

The Five Emperors, also known in local contexts as the Five Blessings or Five Spirits, are closely tied to ideas of epidemic control, boundary protection, and communal safety.

Their power lies in transformation: figures once associated with disease become guardians who patrol, contain, and expel harm.

II

Prince Figures

The New Visual Icons of Youshen

Young, charismatic, and deeply tied to online circulation

Contemporary Icon

Prince Zhao

Son of Marshal Zhao in local imagination

With side-parted hair, black-and-gold robes, and a cool demeanor, Prince Zhao has become one of the most recognizable Youshen figures in recent digital circulation.

Lineage Figure

Prince Zhang

Connected to the Zhang Yuanbo lineage

The prince figures show how Youshen personifies divine families, attendants, and younger presences through a language that feels both traditional and current.

III

Seventh and Eighth Lords

Underworld Messengers

A paired image of contrast, warning, and ritual authority

Seventh Lord

General Xie

Tall, pale, and visually elongated, the Seventh Lord carries an unmistakable underworld presence.

Eighth Lord

General Fan

Dark-faced and heavy in bearing, the Eighth Lord completes the pair through stark visual contrast.
